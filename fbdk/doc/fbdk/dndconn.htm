Drag-and-Drop Connection Editing

Creating [Deleting] connections

1. While holding down the Alt key (Ctrl for non-Windows™ systems), click and drag on an FB data, event or plug output.

2. Drag the target over the data, event or socket input.

3. Release the mouse button and the connection is made.

  • This works for data, event and adapter connections in a function block diagram (FBD), as well as transitions in an Execution Control Chart (ECC).
  • Invalid connections are automatically rejected:
    • Data to event or adapter.
    • Incompatible data or adapter types.
    • Event to data or adapter.
    • Adapter to event or data.
  • "Auto-routing" is performed when an input is to the left of the associated output:
    • Event connections are routed "up and over."
    • Data and adapter connections are routed "down and under."

Deleting and Re-routing [Creating] connections

A connection can be selected in the worksheet by a mouse click on the center (vertical) section of a three-segment line, on one of the three center sections of a five-segment line, or on the transition condition of an EC transition. The connection can then be deleted with the Alt+Del keychord, or the selected segment can be dragged. The connection can also be deleted, or changed back and forth between 3- and 5-segment representations, by a pop-up menu obtained by right-clicking on an appropriate section.

1. A connection is selected. 2. The connection is converted to 5-segment style.
3. The connection is re-routed. 4. The connection is deleted.
