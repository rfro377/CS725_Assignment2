Sensor and Actuator Semantics

Sensors

  • SORT SOL RETURN: +24V when the sort solenoid [2] is in the retracted position. [Map]
  • METAL PEG DETECT: +24V when a metal peg is in the sortation area [2].
    • NOTE 1 - When a peg is present, this signal is typically asserted before the first assertion of the SORT AREA DETECT signal, but may be asserted after SORT AREA DETECT if the latter sensor is not properly aligned.
    • NOTE 2 - When a peg is present, this signal will always be asserted before the first negation of SORT AREA DETECT. [Map]
  • SORT AREA DETECT: +24V when either a metal peg or a ring is in the sortation area[2].
    • NOTE 1 - This signal is asserted upon entry of a ring or peg to the sortation area. The signal is de-asserted while the hole of a ring or central cylinder of a peg passes through the detection area, then asserted again as the trailing edge of the ring or peg passes.
    • NOTE 2 - When this sensor is properly aligned, this signal will be asserted following the rising edge of METAL PEG DETECT when an peg is present; however, misalignment may cause it to be asserted later.
    • NOTE 3 - When this sensor is properly aligned, a ring will be in an appropriate position to be ejected by the SORT SOLENOID when this signal is asserted. Misalignment of this sensor may cause the ejector to jam in front of a ring if the SORT SOLENOID is activated by the logical condition (SORT AREA DETECT) & NOT (METAL PEG DETECT), which may bend the ejector shaft due to the high force exerted by the chain conveyor. [Map]
  • RING PRESENT: +24V when one or more rings are in the chute [3]. [Map]
  • INDEX CAM OPEN: +24V when the index cam is in position to drop a ring into the hopper [3,4]. NOTE - There is a significant region of overlap where both INDEX CAM OPEN and INDEX CAM CLOSED are asserted during activation and de-activation of ROTARY SOLENOID. [Map]
  • INDEX CAM CLOSED: +24V when the index cam is in position to receive and hold a ring [3]. See NOTE on INDEX CAM OPEN. [Map]
  • ASSEMBLY HOPPER FULL: +24V when the assembly hopper [4] contains at least one ring. [Map]
    • NOTE 1 - The assembly hopper can hold two rings; however, this may cause a jam in the assembly operation.
    • NOTE 2 - This sensor may "bounce" during the assembly operation.
  • PEG PRESENT: 0V when a peg is present at the entrance to the assembly area; +24V otherwise [4]. [Map]
  • PEG AT RING CHUTE: 0V when a peg or ring is entering or leaving the area under the ring chute; +24V otherwise [4]. [Map]
    • NOTE - This signal may be at +24V momentarily when the peg is directly opposite the retroreflective detector if the latter is improperly calibrated.
  • ASSEMBLY DETECT: +24V when a peg or ring, or both, are in the inspection area [6] [Map]
    • NOTE - When a ring assembled to a peg is passing:
      1. This signal follows the RING ASSEMBLED signal in time.
      2. There is some overlap between the periods of assertion of the two signals.
      3. This signal remains asserted for some time following the de-assertion of the RING ASSEMBLED signal.
  • RING ASSEMBLED:+24V when a ring on a peg is entering the inspection area [6]. See NOTE on ASSEMBLY DETECT. [Map]
  • REJECT AREA DETECT: +24V when an object is in the reject area [6]. [Map]
  • REJECT SOL RETURN: +24V when the reject solenoid [6] is retracted. [Map]
  • START: +24V when the START pushbutton is pressed.
  • STOP: +24V when the STOP pushbutton is up (not pressed); 0V when it is down (pressed).
  • EMERGENCY STOP 24V: 24VDC energizing power for output circuits when the EMERGENCY STOP button is closed (up); otherwise an open circuit.
  • 24V: 24VDC energizing power for input circuits.

Actuators

  • CHAIN CONVEYOR: Drive power to the chain (feed) conveyor, +24VDC fused at 160mA.[Map]
  • SORT SOLENOID: Activates the sort solenoid [2]. [Map]
  • ROTARY SOLENOID: Activates the rotary solenoid [3]. See NOTE on INDEX CAM OPEN. [Map]
  • REJECT SOLENOID: Activates the reject solenoid [6]. [Map]
  • BELT CONVEYOR: Drive power to the belt (assembly) conveyor, +24VDC fused at 160mA.[Map]
  • GROUND: This connection is interrupted (on the actuator signal connector only) when the EMERGENCY STOP button is depressed.
